ABOUT ME

Computer Science, Machine Learning

I'M Cynthia Habonimana

A computer scientist specializing in machine learning

Cynthia is originally from Burundi, East Africa. She believes that it is particularly important for women to be present in the tech industry, as women have unique and brilliant perspectives to contribute. Her current research interests are in the fields of econometrics, health, computer vision, and natural language processing. Cynthia is a graduate of the Computer Science and Engineering program at California State University (Long Beach Campus), where she majored in Computer Science. She is also a graduate of the Mathematics department and the Physics department at Cypress College, where she earned associate degrees in Mathematics and in Physics in 2016.

Professional Experience

A summary of recent professional experience

Cynthia was a mentee in Google AI’s research mentorship cohort, working with Hugo Larochelle on various educational projects in deep learning. She conducted research in Summer 2019 at Cornell University, working with Rediet Abebe and Jon Kleinberg to analyze the dynamics of poverty using Poverty Tracker, a dataset documenting poverty and disadvantage in New York City. In Spring 2019, she worked as a AI Curriculum Developper at AI 4 ALL, an organization working on opening doors to artificial intelligence for underrepresented talent through education and mentorship. In Summer 2018, she interned in San Francisco as a data engineer at Macy’s Technology, a leading
e-commerce company and the largest U.S. department store company by retail sales. During her internship in San Francisco, joined the World Blockchain Hackathon and won the first Prize with her team BTrue.
